Dear students, 
These are some Final Project examples:
* Medical Imaging: Tumor segmentation with U-Net + GradCAM explainability
* LLM Application: Domain-specific chatbot with RAG (legal, medical, technical support)
* Multi-Modal System: Visual search engine for e-commerce with CLIP
* Generative AI: Style transfer system or text-to-image application
* Object Detection: Custom detector for specific domain (wildlife, manufacturing defects)
* Document Understanding: Multi-modal LLM for invoice/receipt processing
* Fine-Tuned LLM: Specialized model for code generation or creative writing

You may also follow a different route, for example:
* Identify a running or recent shared-task / scientific challenge and propose a (possibly partial) solution;
* Identify a paper and implement a lighter version of the proposed model, or run ablation studies on the model (if not already included on the paper), or implement a strong ML baseline that the authors left out.

You may select one of these examples or use them as a starting point to define your own proposal. Either way, you must confirm that you can find data and that you can access computational resources that allow training a model.
You may/should consider light(er) models and focus on optimizing efficacy/efficiency tradeoff. The project will be evaluated in terms of the architecture and training options and their justification, not on the final results. Note however that results should achieve a reasonable and/or justifiable level when considering the problem and model type/size, and that error analysis should be included.


As a reminder, the 2-3 page proposal must include:
* Problem Statement: What are you solving? Why is it interesting/important?
* Dataset: What data will you use? Source, size, preprocessing needed. Show sample data.
* Methodology: What models/techniques? Why these choices? Architecture diagram.
* Evaluation Plan: What metrics? Baseline comparisons. Success criteria.
* Ethics & Limitations: Potential biases. Limitations of approach. Responsible use considerations

Use the ACM SIG conference template (single column, you can/should remove the image on the first page).
